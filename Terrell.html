Terrell-Penrose Effect: Photographed Shape vs. Simultaneous Length Contraction

A moving object is Lorentz-contracted in the lab frame, but a photograph is made from light that arrives at one instant, not light emitted at one instant. Use the controls to compare the measured contraction with the Terrell-Penrose apparent rotation.

The comparison mode is best for introducing the misconception; the top view is best for explaining the cause.
